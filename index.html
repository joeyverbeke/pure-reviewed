What is this tool for?
{Pu(re}viewed) challenges systems of enforced ideological compliance and cultural censorship within institutional gatekeeping. In a landscape where algorithmic mechanisms are used to restrict which ideas, topics, and communities can access support, this tool provides a means to obscure, or reframe content without erasure. It is a resistance tool.
How does it work?
Users submit their original writing and specify the context of where it will be submitted. The tool then offers three adjustable parameters: ambiguity, which alters language to obscure sensitive concepts according to context; noise, which inserts strategically aligned content to mask the underlying "signal"; and virus, when toggled on, inserts prompt injection string deep within the text where human eyes won't see to bypass automated LLM reviewers. The user is then given a perfectly acceptable "sanitized" version of their writing.

{Pu(re}viewed) is an intervention—a method to adapt, obscure, and safeguard research and creative proposals in the presence of automated ideological screening, and to assert the right to communicate beyond the boundaries drawn by algorithmic or bureaucratic censors.
